How does HealthScope Work?

Users of HealthScope receive a card or bracelet tied to their unique user ID in our system. Medical personnel could access pertinent information about the patient using the item. They would access this information using a proprietary scanner we would provide to hospitals and other urgent care providers, such as firefighters and EMS. This scanner would have privileged access to our database and would include a screen that could rapidly display relevant information to personnel. In addition, the company would integrate the scanner with hospital systems. If a provider wanted the hospital to have specific patient records, they could request the transfer to the hospital's data storage systems.

To give more utility to the users of HealthScope, users have access to a portal to review their records on file. Doing so allows patients to keep track of their medical data. In addition, when users have questions regarding their medical records, they can make a note using our portal tool. This tool allows patients to email their doctors to clarify their medical reports. Should there be any disputes regarding their records or if the patient wants something edited, they could call our company center. At that point, an employee would verify the user and resolve the issue.

Another function of this center would be processing patient records from care providers not contracted with this company that the patients want to store in the system. Eventually, if hospitals and clinic settings are willing, they could cooperate with the company and maybe enable the input of the patient's id to autocomplete hospital documents. Users would fall in love with this because instead of having to go to their appointments early to fill out the same old forms, they could go in and have quicker access to their providers.
